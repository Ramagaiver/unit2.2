Browse geographic data from the MormonPlaces database, based on periodized settlement records from 1847 to 1900. Kernel density isolines surrounding Mormon settlements act as a base for future investigation into Mormon interactions with Native bands. However, because these boundaries are derived from density analysis and historical spatial modeling, they are subject to interpretive variance, particularly in regions with sparse documentation or transient site occupation, and are best regarded as hypothetical estimates of territorial extent. As you sequence through the settlement expansion, observe the correlation between Mormon density and environmental features like rivers. Ask yourself: What drove the leap-frogging pattern of certain settlements? How did the site extent from Mormon settlements physically restrict the seasonal mobility of the Northern Shoshone or Ute bands? This map serves as a prompt to visually hypothesize the resource competition (rather than purely the proximity of settlemenets) that dictated the territorial dispossession of natives in Utah.
